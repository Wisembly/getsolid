Welcome to Solid

This guide will help you get your first meeting off the ground. We\'ll also offer tips for taking notes or reminding attendees.

If you have any questions that are not mentioned in this guide, please contact us: contact@wisembly.com

Now, let\'s get started.

Prepare a meeting

Once you\'ve created your account, Solid automatically retrieves all your agenda information from your Google Calendar.

Remember: get your participants prepared beforehand is the foundation of a successful meeting.

To prepare a meeting on Solid, follow these steps:

  1. Choose your next meeting, in the weekly calendar view
  2. Add a goal: let your attendees know in one sentence why you\'re asking them to meet
  3. Add the few topics you want to see covered during your meeting in the "Agenda" section
  4. Click on "Send reminder" to remind your attendees to confirm the meeting or to add topics directly on the agenda
  5. Write a personal message along with the reminder (optional) and click "Send"

Your meeting background color will now turn green. It means that it is "solid" enough to be held.

Take notes during a meeting

As your meeting starts, the timer will start. It will indicate the time remaining before the end of the meeting.

If you started late, you can click on the "reset meeting" just below the timer. Thus, the timer restarts from scratch.

To take notes quickly:
  • If you have not done it before, start by adding the main topics you want to be covered during the meeting by clicking on "add an new agenda item"
  • Click "add a note" to start writing down your notes related to an agenda item
  • Just press the "enter" key to move to the next note
Keyboard shortcuts

We\'ve added some handy keyboards shortcuts to make editing your content even easier and tag your notes:

  • start a note with ":t" to add a task
  • start a note with ":d" to add a decision
  • start a note with ":p" to add an open issue

Ex: :t @mark send the report to the team

Send meeting minutes

Allowing you participants to instantly receive the minutes of your meeting is a great way to involve them and show them how well you organize meetings.

  1. When your meeting is finished, click "end meeting". The timer will stop and a "send summary" button will appear. Once you are satisfied with the report, click "send summary" to share it.
  2. Write a personal message along with the summary (optional) and click "Send".

All participants will receive a detailed summary of the meeting\'s outcomes and also a list of their personal upcoming tasks.
